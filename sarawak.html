About Sarawak

Sarawak is a Malaysian state located on the northwest coast of Borneo. Known for its rich cultural heritage and vast rainforests, Sarawak is often referred to as the "Land of the Hornbills." The state is a haven for eco-tourism, offering travelers the chance to explore pristine jungles, traditional longhouses, and vibrant indigenous cultures.

"Sarawak is a journey into the heart of Borneo, where culture and nature coexist in harmony."


Tourism Expert Jane Smith

From the ancient caves of Mulu National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, to the traditional longhouses of the Iban people, Sarawak offers a unique cultural and natural experience. Whether you're exploring the wildlife in its dense rainforests or immersing yourself in the local traditions, Sarawak promises an adventure filled with discovery and wonder.

Featured Tourism Places

Bako National Park

This national park contains almost all variety of Bornean vegetation, abundant wildlife, secluded beaches, trekking trails, jungle streams and waterfalls. One must not miss the chance to sight the star of Bako National Park, the Proboscis Monkey.

Siniawan Old Town

Siniawan old town, located in the Bau District comes alive at night on the weekends, transforming it into one of the most memorable food experience you can have. Admission to the old town is free.

Tua Pek Kong Temple

Tua Pek Kong Temple is a Chinese temple situated near the waterfront of Kuching, Sarawak, Malaysia, opposite the Chinese History Museum. It is the oldest temple in the city and formed a part of the Kuching Heritage Trail.

Kuching Waterfront

It's particularly entertaining at night when street food stalls, eateries, and vendors are at their liveliest, and the Darul Hana Music Fountain lights up the river in a blaze of color. Many Kuching sightseeing tours visit the waterfront, and it's also a great spot to join a Sarawak River cruise.

Jong's Crocodile Farm

Seeking a thrilling experience? Look no further than Jong's Crocodile Farm, home to over 1,000 crocodiles. Watch nature’s “bad boys” in action as they leap several feet out of the water during feeding time. But don't worry, you'll be watching from a safe distance.

Miri-Sibuti Coral Reefs National Park

Miri is a popular dive destination due to an abundance of pristine patch reefs. The Miri-Sibuti Coral Reefs National Park, lying at depths ranging from 7 to 30 metres has an average visibility of 10 to 30 metres. There are also some interesting wreck dives.
