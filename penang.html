About Penang

Penang is a Malaysian state located on the northwest coast of Peninsular Malaysia. Known as the "Pearl of the Orient," Penang is celebrated for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant street food scene, and stunning natural landscapes.

"Penang is where history and modernity coexist, offering a vibrant tapestry of experiences."


Tourism Expert Jane Smith

From the historic streets of George Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, to the tranquil beaches of Batu Ferringhi, Penang offers a unique blend of the old and new. Whether you're a foodie eager to explore its famous street food or a culture enthusiast drawn to its colonial architecture and colorful street art, Penang has something to captivate every traveler.

A natural amalgamation of pleasant weather, luxurious vegetation, breathtaking views, colonial vibe and the foresty landscape is what you call as Penang Hill. Located in Air Itam suburb, Penang Hill is a lovely little hill station in Penang Island, Malaysia.

Kek Lok Si Temple

Situated on the hilltop at Air Itam near Penang Hill, Kek Lok Si is one of the largest Chinese Buddhist temples in Southeast Asia. Comprising of monasteries, temples, prayer halls, and gardens, the temple complex is a chief pilgrimage centre for Buddhists from Philippines and Singapore.
